Corpora Caelestia

data visualization of satellites in geostationary (GEO) orbit
Watch generative mini satellites orbit your mouse

View It! Dataset

Corpora Caelestia is a visualisation of satellites in geostationary(GEO) orbit that were launched over the past three years (2019-21). It showcases the inequalities between the world's nations using satellites as an indicator of power and status.
Each satellite is procedurally generated based on factors like purpose, year of launch, etc. and mapped to its location in space using actual satellite position data. The satellites connect to their respective owner country and launch site.

There are 7 launch sites and 14 countries who own satellites. This demonstrates the hierarchy of nations in the Space Race because geostationary satellites are the hardest to launch due to being the highest distance from the earth's crust. Countries owning launch sites and satellites gives them monopoly over the communication they support and the data they collect.
Corpora Caelestia questions the need for a Space Race and the monetisation of outer space for furthering global inequality.
