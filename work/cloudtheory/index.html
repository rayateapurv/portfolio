Description

I worked as a research assistant on Cloud Theory, working with Parsons game design professor Colleen Macklin. Cloud Theory is a relaxing videogame where you lay on the ground and look at clouds. Every once in a while you or your friend might see a shape in the clouds, which sparks conversation about everything from the origin of poodles to the deepest mysteries of life. Cloud Theory is part of a research project called Machine Unlearning, an exploration of the ways in which intelligence is within the mind of the beholder, not the algorithm.

Role

Unity Developer

  • Implemented a live connection between a fine-tuned GPT-Neo model hosted on HuggingFace and Unity.
  • Coded text-to-speech dialogue in Unity using the RT-Voice Plugin.
  • Designed a custom system for procedural dialogue using Unity scriptable objects.

Outcomes

  • A demo of this game was presented by Colleen Macklin at WordHack NYC 2023.
  • It is an ongoing project at the PETLab, a game research group at Parsons.
